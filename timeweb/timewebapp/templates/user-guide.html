      Introduction

      Getting started
      Welcome! TimeWeb's user guide documents each of its features, quirks, and edge cases. Reading this isn't required, but doing so will clear up confusion you may have and greatly help with your user experience.

      In this user guide, important notes and FAQs are marked with a label. For example:

      Why doesn't the internal date change after midnight? This behavior isn't a bug nor a glitch, but is actually intentional. Changing the date at midnight will result in showing the work for the next day despite the current work day not being finished. So, the internal date only changes to the next at the hardcoded value of 4 AM.
      If you came here because you're confused about something specific, there's a good chance you'll find your answer in either the "Table of contents," which lists every category, or "Important labels," which lists every note and FAQ in this guide.

      What is TimeWeb and why should I use it?
      TimeWeb is a free, online time manager that prioritizes, sorts, and lists each of your daily school or work assignments. Unlike an ordinary planner, each of your inputted assignments' work is split up and neatly graphed over the days until they are due. Then, they are automatically prioritized so you will always know which assignment to do, when to do it, and how long it will take. TimeWeb's main focus is on customizability and convenience. As we'll get to later on in this guide, TimeWeb supports many advanced parameters that can be configured for you and your work style.

      A few extra things to help with TimeWeb's credibility:
      • TimeWeb scored an average of 8.3/10 in overall user satisfaction in its incomplete beta
      • TimeWeb has been supported and received well by my school's principal
      • TimeWeb is open source
      If you would like, take a look at TimeWeb's YouTube demo to get an idea of what you should expect your home page to look like.
