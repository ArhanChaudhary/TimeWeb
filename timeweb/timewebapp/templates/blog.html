Ideation
My journey creating TimeWeb began on October, 2019. I was an 8th grade student at middle school in Fremont, California. In my science class, my teacher played a motivational Ted Talk about procrastination. I had seen the video before, in fact. It featured a simple graph of daily work to complete over time for an assignment. Though I found it entertaining, I didn't think much of it. However, after rewatching the video in class, I had an idea: what if you could code a similar graph system for your own work?
Humble beginnings
Although I barely knew anything about computers and had a very basic prior understanding of programming, I spent a Friday night learning how to use pygame, a python-based graphical library, not knowing anything else to use. I managed to finish the first ever prototype of my command line program. Not knowing basic code organization practices, my code back then was unbelievably bad, almost impressively bad. Web development
I reached out to a close family friend about my project, and he was genuinely impressed. He recommended me to broaden my vision of my program to the web, specifically with the Django framework. Confident and determined, I went home and searched up how to use Django. It dawned upon me just how little I knew about web development. Some terms I googled include "Convert python to javascript" and "Upload program to Django". In distress, I asked my dad for help, so he sat down to help me set up a basic Django project. His main goal wasn't for me to learn Django, but rather to learn how to learn. For the next week, we went through the documentation and learned the basics of Django architecture. I had finally reached a comfortable level of knowledge to begin building a web application.
